Running a business solo means you wear every hat. You're the sales team, the marketer, the customer service rep, the accountant, and the delivery person — sometimes all on the same day.

AI doesn't solve all of that. But the right tools can meaningfully reduce how much of your time gets eaten by low-value work. Here are five I recommend to nearly every solopreneur I work with.

## 1. Claude or ChatGPT — Your AI Thinking Partner

**Best for:** Writing, brainstorming, research, summarizing, drafting

Both Claude and ChatGPT are general-purpose AI assistants that you can have a conversation with. Think of them as a brilliant, tireless colleague who can help you:

- Draft emails, proposals, and social posts
- Summarize documents and articles
- Brainstorm ideas and work through problems
- Explain complex concepts in plain language

**My recommendation:** Start with Claude for longer documents and nuanced writing. Try ChatGPT for quick back-and-forths.

**Free tier available:** Yes (both)

---

## 2. Notion AI — Your Business Brain Upgrade

**Best for:** Note-taking, project management, knowledge base, AI-assisted writing

If you already use Notion (and if you don't, you should consider it), the built-in AI features are genuinely useful. You can:

- Summarize meeting notes instantly
- Generate action items from a brain dump
- Write first drafts directly inside your workspace
- Ask questions about your own documents

For solopreneurs who need to capture and organize a lot of information, this is a game-changer.

**Free tier available:** Notion is free; AI features are a paid add-on

---

## 3. Zapier or Make — Automation Without Code

**Best for:** Connecting apps, automating repetitive tasks, building workflows

These platforms let you build automation workflows that connect different apps — no coding required. With AI features now built in, you can:

- Automatically send new leads to your CRM and follow up by email
- Post to social media when you publish a blog post
- Route customer inquiries to the right place
- Summarize form submissions and email them to yourself

If you find yourself doing the same sequence of steps across multiple apps more than once a week, there's probably a Zapier workflow that can handle it.

**Free tier available:** Yes (both, with limits)

---

## 4. Otter.ai or Fireflies — Never Take Meeting Notes Again

**Best for:** Recording and transcribing meetings, capturing client calls

These tools automatically transcribe your calls and meetings, then use AI to summarize key points and action items. The result:

- You can be fully present in meetings instead of furiously taking notes
- You have a searchable record of every conversation
- Action items are captured and easy to share

Particularly valuable if you're doing sales calls or client consultations.

**Free tier available:** Yes (both, with recording limits)

---

## 5. Canva AI — Design Without a Designer

**Best for:** Social media graphics, presentations, marketing materials

Canva's AI features have transformed what's possible for non-designers. You can now:

- Generate images from text descriptions
- Remove backgrounds from photos instantly
- Resize designs for different platforms in one click
- Use AI to suggest design layouts

The result isn't always perfect, but it's usually good enough — and fast.

**Free tier available:** Yes (AI features are part of Canva Pro)

---

## The Bigger Picture

These five tools cover writing, thinking, automation, communication, and design — most of the key areas where solopreneurs lose time every week.

You don't need to use all five at once. Pick one area where you're currently losing the most time and start there. The goal is to build habits, not to implement everything overnight.
